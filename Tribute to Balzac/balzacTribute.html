Honoré de Balzac

Splendors and miseries of the greatest french novelist

Hard worker and having a deep knowledge of the human soul, Balzac wrote an immense litterary work: The Human Comedy. Unhappy in business, he accumulated debts and commercial fails.
Desperatly in love with a married woman, he could only envisioned a futur with her few month before his death.

Here a time line of Honoré de Balzac's life:

  • 1799 - Born in Tours, France.
  • 1814 - Moved to Paris with is familly.
  • 1819 - Passed the Law Baccalauréat and the same year, decided to become a writer.
  • 1820-1826 - Successively journalist, editor, publisher and was even tempted by a career in politics.
  • 1826 - Went bankrupt as a printer and began to accumulute debts.
  • 1831 - Published The Skin of Shagreen.
  • 1834 - Began an relationship with Ewelina Hańska, polish noblewoman and love of his life.
  • 1837 - Wrote Lost Illusions.
  • 1838 - Traveled to Sardinia in the hopes of reprocessing the slag from the Roman mines there. He lost all the money he invested and extremly increased his debts.
  • 1840 - Founded a publication, the Revue Parisienne. Another business fail.
  • 1847 - Travelled to Ukraine to join Ewelina Hanska.
  • 1850 - Married Ewelina Hańska in Ukraine.
  • 1850 - Dies in Paris at the age of 51.
